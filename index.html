Overdose (released Sep 2022)

Starting off strong here... I've always found it quite interesting that I actually didn't discover Natori through his most popular song. When I eventually listened to "Overdose", I could understand what had made it so popular (and especially on a site like TikTok, which hosts short-form content that reuse trending audio clips). Natori himself stated he carefully created the song after analyzing popular trends in music at the time, and it clearly didn't go to waste. However, I must say that when I look at all of the works Natori's released so far, it really comes off as a song I would vibe with but not really recognize. I think what makes Overdose so formulaically popular is also a drawback, since that causes it to blend in with all the other generic hits that are popular for sharing the same features in their music.

Love Story x Terminal (released Dec 2023)

I could've sworn these two were released before that-- no, afterwards?

Anyways, please allow me to introduce my favorite song pairing, "Love Story" and "Terminal", both by Natori. "Love Story" is followed by "Terminal", and they share the story of two cursed lovers who share a tragic and doomed fate. While this may sound like your run-of-the-mill Romeo and Juliet shtick, however, the symbolism and motifs in both the lyrics and the music videos shared between the two songs is what really sold me. Even the colors tell a story; one of warmth, of love, and of loss. I really don't feel like my words could do much justice to them, purely because of the emotional complexity the songs carry, so I'd encourage you to listen to them for yourself.

SPEED (released April 2025)

"SPEED" is a fast-paced song, and, like many of Natori's other works, has lyrics that are raw and carry deep meaning. Usually though, whenever I remember the official music video, the meaning is overpowered by the silly premise of a kid overcoming all the odds to make it to the restroom on time! I'd still consider it a good song, but I have to admit that the meaning of the lyrics escapes me when I listen to it.

Cult. (released November 2023)

My first impression of this song was actually pretty negative. I think one day, this song just clicked. It's now my favorite song by Natori, and this wouldn't be my fan website if it didn't have a section about "Cult". The lyrics are emotionally heavy, as always, yet what really draws me to this song is the melody. It's catchy, upbeat, and I think it sounds like someone trying to send a last heartfelt and hopeful message to the world. It reminds me of myself in a way, and the way I want to live and carry myself. I doubt I'll lose my love for this song anytime soon!

Propose (released Jun 2025)

Would it come as a surprise to find that "Overdose" was eventually relieved of its position as Natori's most popular song? "Propose" is a cute song about the small anxieties one might face as they ready themselves to, hopefully, propose to the one they love. It's got a catchy chorus, and has a sweet melody to it, which makes it a prime choice for a trending audio. I actually like it a bit more than "Overdose", though, since the official music video is super adorable, and the song has a distinct feel to it.

Friday Night (released Mar 2023)

"Friday Night" was the first song of Natori's that I listened to. It comes off as slow, yet hypnotizing, which drew me to the artist even further. The magic here is truly in Natori's vocals, at least in my opinion. His voice is bold, yet unforceful, which really embodies the lyrics of this song perfectly.To describe it further, I'd say his voice in "Friday Night" could put me to sleep, in the best possible meaning of that phrase!

Fall Apart [Cover] (released Apr 2025)

I don't know what it is about this song that makes me love it so much! It's rather short, and doesn't have a music video. The bridge is pretty catchy, but apart from that, it could really blend in as any other Natori song. My best guess is that I think "Fall Apart" has all the core points of a Natori song; it embodies his vibe perfectly.

Melodrama and Metrocity (released Aug and Sept 2024)

These songs were the result of a collab between Natori and another popular J-Pop artist, "imase". "Melodrama" came first, and has a slow, late-night vibe to it. It's also very easy to sing along to, especially with its simple yet beautiful chorus. "Metrocity" came next. It's much more upbeat, but rocks a similarly mesmerising chorus that's guarenteed to get stuck in your head. The music video for this one is really cute, because Imase showed up to act in-person, but since Natori tries not to show his face, he was animated alongside Imase. Natori also put in a speedraw of him and Imase, which was super cute!

Eureka (released Jun 2023)

You ever get that feeling of being entranced with something you don't fully understand? Yeah, that's me with this song. Thing is, the lyrics of "Eureka" aren't even that complicated. But there's just so many different ways you could interpret them (and all of them hit me straight in the feels!) The song is incredibly poetic, emotional-- I'd say J-rock?-- and I'd definetely reccomend it to anyone. Might want some tissues with those English translations though.

What is ahead of the tunnel? (released Oct 2025)

This is Natori's latest song, and the choruses have only gotten catchier! I actually don't really understand the lyrics of this one (seems like it tells a specific story but I'm not sure), but the music makes up for it. I didn't like it at first but it definitely stuck.

If you made it this far, you're really awesome! Thanks for sticking with me till the end. I'd say you deserved a break... up for listening to Natori as you rewind?
